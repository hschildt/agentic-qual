# Sycophancy Codebook - Presidential Transitions Study

**Last Updated**: December 8, 2025
**Documents Coded**: 17 interviews across 4 transitions (Ford: 2, Carter: 6, Reagan: 1, Clinton: 8)
**Progress**: Comprehensive hierarchical codebook with 300+ distinct codes

## Overview
This codebook contains descriptive and analytical codes emerging from grounded theory analysis of presidential transition interviews, focused on sycophancy, its antecedents, manifestations, consequences, and protective factors.

---

## 1. MANIFESTATIONS OF SYCOPHANCY

### 1.1 Verbal/Behavioral Sycophancy
- **slavishly-doing-will**: VP "slavishly doing the will of his master" (Hartmann/Ford)
- **yes-sir-general**: Automatic compliance - "everybody would say 'Yes sir, General'" (Hartmann on Reagan)
- **mirroring-leader**: Staff resembling leaders in behavior - "models of themselves" (Hartmann)
- **approval-seeking**: Plans written to please approvers vs. achieve results (White/Ford, Griscom/Reagan)
- **showboating**: Cabinet meetings breeding performance to impress president (McLarty/Clinton)
- **sycophantic-journalist**: Trading editorial independence for presidential access (Jenkins/Carter on Trudy Feldman)
- **pre-vetting-stories**: Journalist letting WH review/change articles for access (Jenkins/Carter)

### 1.2 Institutional/Structural Sycophancy
- **hierarchical-deference**: Structured compliance in pyramidal organizations (Hartmann, Griscom)
- **ceremonial-leadership**: Leader "reigns, presides" while staff executes (Hartmann on Reagan)
- **invisible-service**: Speechwriter norm - "function of a ghost is to be invisible" (Hartmann)
- **delegated-enforcement**: Chief of staff as "son of a bitch" absorbing blame (Hartmann)
- **closed-system**: Regan's aides as "the mice" - hierarchical sycophancy (Griscom/Reagan)
- **hierarchical-approval-systems**: Haldeman's action memos creating approval-seeking (White/Ford)
- **gatekeeping**: Lindsay controlling access to Clinton, protective sycophancy (Mikva, Panetta)
- **tribal-fear**: Staff circle protecting president from himself (Mikva/Clinton)

### 1.3 Performance Sycophancy
- **performance-over-substance**: Looking good on paper more than effectiveness (White/Ford, Fetig/Clinton)
- **hours-competition**: Pride in overwork as display of commitment (White/Ford, Lipshutz/Carter)
- **self-imposed-pressure**: Perfectionism driving sycophantic overwork (White)
- **extreme-overwork**: Rubenstein sleeping on couch, needing vending machines (Lipshutz/Carter)
- **performative-commitment**: Displaying dedication through suffering (White, Lipshutz)
- **hours-as-loyalty**: Using extreme hours to demonstrate devotion (Lipshutz Rubenstein example)

### 1.4 Access and Proximity Sycophancy
- **proximity-to-power-seeking**: Choosing West Wing for status over function (Jenkins/Carter)
- **access-seeking-universal**: "They all want to meet with him...There's nothing like a meeting with President" (Jenkins)
- **meeting-with-president-currency**: Access as most valued commodity (Jenkins)
- **brzezinski-rasputin**: Leveraging exclusive 7:15am meeting for "inordinate control" (Jenkins/Carter)
- **job-ownership**: Staff acting like they "own" positions, can't leave (Griscom/Reagan)
- **intoxication-with-proximity**: Power "goes to their head" (Griscom)
- **west-wing-prestige**: Status-seeking over functional appropriateness (Jenkins)

### 1.5 Communication/Press Sycophancy
- **selecting-favorable-press**: Choosing journalists "friendly to your side" (Jenkins/Carter)
- **access-for-compliance**: Trading access for favorable coverage (Jenkins)
- **access-as-currency**: Granting interviews to shape favorable coverage (Jenkins)
- **avoiding-hostile-journalists**: "No Press Secretary would set up interview with Maureen Dowd" (Jenkins)
- **friend-vs-adversary**: Strategic selection based on expected friendliness (Jenkins)
- **interview-access-trade**: Access for editorial compliance (Jenkins on Feldman)
- **serving-presidents-interest**: Coverage shaped to favor administration (Jenkins)

### 1.6 Information Control
- **information-shaping**: Manipulating information flow to influence decisions (McLarty explicitly rejects)
- **sunday-show-clearance-required**: Must get permission before public appearances (Jenkins/Carter)
- **unauthorized-appearance-problems**: Assistant Secretary causing "problems" without clearance (Jenkins)
- **message-control**: Coordinated talking points across administration (Panetta/Clinton, Jenkins/Carter)
- **top-down-message-control**: President monitoring unauthorized media appearances (Jenkins)
- **withholding-information**: Selective disclosure to shape outcomes (Mikva/Clinton)

### 1.7 Campaign Loyalty as Sycophancy Currency
- **campaign-entitlement**: Early supporters feeling they "deserve jobs" (McLarty, Griscom, Lipshutz)
- **spoils-system**: "I was there; I picked President...I should have control" (Griscom/Reagan)
- **loyalty-rewards**: Positions distributed based on campaign service (McLarty, Lipshutz)
- **patronage-logic**: "They deserve jobs" mentality (McLarty)
- **resentment-of-washington-insiders**: Campaign people resisting experienced advisers (Griscom)

## 2. ANTI-SYCOPHANTIC BEHAVIORS

### 2.1 Principled Resistance
- **refusing-assignment**: Declining to work for problematic leader (White re: Colson)
- **quit-threat**: Willingness to resign rather than comply (White, Mikva/Clinton)
- **speaking-up**: Calling out problematic actors (White re: Liddy as "crazy")
- **warning-signs**: Recognizing and reporting dangerous patterns (White)
- **speaking-truth-to-power**: Being "extremely direct" despite presidential temper (McLarty/Clinton)
- **refusing-collaboration**: Won't work with dangerous people (White on Liddy)
- **principled-stand**: Drawing ethical lines (White refusing Colson)
- **refusing-blind-defense**: Not defending president when wrong (Mikva/Clinton)

### 2.2 Strategic Independence
- **complementary-staffing**: Reagan hiring Baker - different from himself (Hartmann, Griscom)
- **facilitation-vs-direction**: Choosing to enable rather than control (White, McLarty)
- **balanced-perspective**: Not being career-dependent enables independence (White/Ford)
- **exit-readiness**: "If they didn't want me there the next day, so be it" (White, Griscom)
- **goal-setting**: Entering with specific missions, leaving when accomplished (Griscom/Reagan)
- **mission-orientation**: Focus on objectives not position (Griscom)
- **exit-planning**: Knowing when to leave (Griscom)
- **temporary-role-awareness**: Understanding you don't "own" the job (Griscom)

### 2.3 Integrity-Based Leadership
- **straight-arrow-leadership**: Klein's credibility from trustworthiness (White/Ford)
- **anti-manipulation**: Explicitly rejecting controlling approaches (White, McLarty, Panetta)
- **honesty-as-constraint**: Ford's inability to lie limiting maneuverability (Hartmann)
- **transparency**: Ford's openness vs. Nixon's secretiveness (Hartmann)
- **powell-integrity**: Jody Powell's "no personal agendas," compassion (Jenkins/Carter)
- **honest-broker-ideal**: Fair presentation of information without manipulation (McLarty, Panetta)
- **no-personal-agenda**: Leader serving institution not self (Powell, Panetta)
- **standing-up-for-subordinate**: Protecting vulnerable staff member (Powell per Jenkins)

### 2.4 Consultation and Reality-Checking
- **broad-consultation**: Carter circulating memos seeking multiple perspectives (Lipshutz)
- **soliciting-dissent**: Inviting "sometimes not support" of decisions (Lipshutz)
- **external-advice**: Griscom's weekly informal advisers from both parties (Griscom/Reagan)
- **cross-party-consultation**: Seeking perspectives from Republicans and Democrats (Griscom)
- **avoiding-isolation**: Deliberately bringing in outside voices (McLarty, Griscom)
- **fresh-perspectives**: Preventing insularity through external reality-checks (McLarty, Griscom)
- **perspective-maintenance**: Keeping grounded through diverse input (Griscom)
- **anti-echo-chamber**: Actively preventing groupthink (Lipshutz, Griscom)

### 2.5 Professional Standards
- **journalism-norms**: Outsider status harder to manipulate peers (White on Klein)
- **professional-commitment**: Serving institution over party loyalty (White, Lipshutz)
- **cross-party-cooperation**: White reaching out to Barrio, Ford to Carter (White, Lipshutz)
- **institutional-knowledge-transfer**: Ford people helping Carter people (Lipshutz)
- **learning-from-predecessors**: Valuing institutional memory (Lipshutz, Griscom)
- **bipartisan-professionalism**: Serving office over partisan loyalty (White, Lipshutz)
- **peer-credibility**: Professional background creating respect (White on Klein)

### 2.6 Competence Over Loyalty
- **competence-over-loyalty**: Hiring for ability not just devotion (Nash, Lewis, Fetig)
- **campaign-as-test**: Experience testing competence not just loyalty (Lewis, Nash/Clinton)
- **anti-sycophancy-hiring**: Valuing competence markers not personal devotion (Lewis)
- **campaign-not-hack**: "Being in campaign doesn't mean you're a hack" (Nash)
- **merit-over-loyalty**: Policy objectives over patronage (Lipshutz, Nash)
- **universal-competence**: Good decision-making same everywhere (Lewis)

## 3. ANTECEDENTS OF SYCOPHANCY

### 3.1 Structural/Organizational
- **power-concentration**: "Only one person has the real power" necessitates hierarchy (Hartmann)
- **pyramidal-necessity**: Pyramid structure "works better in government positions" (Hartmann)
- **legitimacy-deficit**: Unelected status (Ford succession) limiting authority (Hartmann)
- **hands-on-vs-delegated**: Organizational model shapes sycophancy dynamics (Griscom)
- **management-models**: Hub-and-spoke vs. pyramid affecting staff behavior (Griscom)
- **hierarchical-approval-systems**: Haldeman's action memos creating approval-seeking (White)
- **no-chief-of-staff-filter**: Carter spokes-of-wheel enabling direct access (Lipshutz)

### 3.2 Individual/Psychological
- **youthful-inexperience**: Young Nixon staffers vulnerable to power corruption (White)
- **power-corruption**: "Imbued with the power" leading to moral compass loss (White)
- **excitement-over-judgment**: Awe overwhelming critical thinking (White)
- **career-dependence**: Needing job for livelihood increases compliance (White)
- **political-ambition**: "Intensely competitive...conspirators" not servants (Hartmann)
- **power-intoxication**: First taste of White House authority "goes to their head" (Griscom)
- **ego**: Staff forgetting they don't "own" the job (Griscom)
- **self-importance**: Losing perspective on temporary nature of role (Griscom)

### 3.3 Campaign and Electoral Dynamics
- **campaign-loyalty-creating-entitlement**: Early supporters feel they "deserve jobs" (McLarty, Griscom)
- **electoral-victory-hubris**: Winning creates sense of invincibility (McLarty)
- **arrogance-ignorance-adrenaline**: Neustadt's formulation of young staff vulnerabilities (McLarty)
- **generational-hubris**: Young campaign staff resenting experienced advisers (Griscom)
- **spoils-system**: Expectation of rewards for campaign loyalty (Griscom)
- **campaign-vs-governing-tension**: Different skills for winning vs. governing (McLarty, Lipshutz)
- **rejecting-wisdom**: Young staff refusing institutional memory (Griscom)

### 3.4 Leadership/Cultural
- **predecessor's-disgrace**: Nixon's Watergate devaluing loyalty to his people (Hartmann)
- **unethical-second-tier**: Colson-type leaders enabling wrongdoing (White, Hartmann)
- **symbolic-rejection**: Ford rejecting Haldeman czar model (Hartmann)
- **moral-character**: President's decency (Ford, Carter) or lack (Nixon) setting tone (White, Mikva)
- **presidential-tone-setting**: Leader's ethics rippling through organization (White, Mikva, Jenkins)
- **clinton-temper**: Emotional reactions staff must navigate (McLarty)
- **managing-temperament**: Dealing with presidential outbursts (McLarty)

### 3.5 Contextual
- **transition-crisis**: No time for transition (Ford) creating staff conflicts (Hartmann)
- **succession-vs-election**: Different legitimacy for succeeded vs. elected presidents (Hartmann)
- **congressional-vs-presidential-culture**: Hill's collaborative norms vs. executive command (Hartmann, Lipshutz)
- **watergate-trauma**: Post-scandal environment requiring rehabilitation (White)
- **hostile-climate**: Investigation/lawsuit threats creating defensive stress (Lewis/Clinton)
- **partisan-warfare**: Constant legal exposure pressuring compliance (Lewis)
- **transparency-weaponization**: Documentation used against administration (Lewis)
- **investigation-threat**: Congressional investigations and lawsuits (Lewis)

### 3.6 Insular Staffing
- **trust-as-paramount**: Prioritizing trust over technical qualifications (Lipshutz/Carter)
- **georgia-mafia**: Loyalty-based insular staffing (Lipshutz, multiple Carter)
- **loyalty-over-expertise**: Personal relationships trumping competence (Lipshutz)
- **campaign-loyalty-required**: Hiring only campaign veterans (Lipshutz, Nash)
- **no-washington-insiders**: Critical mistake of excluding establishment (Lipshutz)
- **insular-hiring**: Selecting only known quantities (Lipshutz)
- **trust-through-shared-history**: Years working together creating assumed understanding (Lipshutz)
- **assumed-understanding**: Long shared history creating implicit norms (Lipshutz)

### 3.7 Institutional Gaps
- **no-preparation**: Zero formal briefing materials or onboarding (Fetig/Clinton)
- **steep-learning-curve**: Taking 6 months to understand role (Jenkins/Carter, Fetig)
- **reinventing-wheel**: Each administration's arrogance refusing to learn (White)
- **institutional-amnesia**: "Never look back" preventing learning (Lewis/Clinton)
- **learning-resistance**: Not wanting predecessors' advice (White)
- **too-late-when-learned**: By time staff understand, administration half over (Jenkins)
- **six-months-to-learn**: Long learning period for basic role understanding (Jenkins)

### 3.8 Polling and Image Consciousness
- **polling-driven**: Constant focus group testing of policies (McCurry/Clinton)
- **appearance-over-substance**: Image-consciousness distorting personnel decisions (Fetig/Clinton)
- **tv-focus**: Administration prioritizing "whether good on TV" (Fetig/Clinton)
- **superficial-criteria**: Looking good vs. being competent (Fetig)
- **poll-driven-decisions**: Letting polls shape policy (McCurry)

## 4. CONSEQUENCES OF SYCOPHANCY

### 4.1 Organizational Dysfunction
- **organizational-inefficiency**: Spokes-of-wheel model proving unworkable (Hartmann)
- **staff-conflict**: Ford vs. Nixon people "brutal war" (Hartmann)
- **incomplete-transition**: Ford Cabinet remained Nixon people throughout (Hartmann)
- **plans-looking-good-vs-working**: Ineffective operations prioritizing appearance (White)
- **three-people-crowded-together**: Proximity-seeking creating inefficiency (Jenkins/Carter)
- **staff-bloat-problem**: Too many people seeking presidential access (Jenkins)
- **lost-institutional-memory**: Wholesale changes losing "core policies" understanding (Griscom)
- **egalitarian-but-messy**: Inclusive decision-making lacking structure (Fetig/Clinton)
- **graduate-school-bull-sessions**: Powell's critique of NSC meetings (Fetig citing Powell)

### 4.2 Ethical/Moral
- **moral-compass-loss**: Watergate participants losing ethical direction (White)
- **self-deception**: Thinking one serves "greater good" while doing harm (White)
- **criminal-behavior**: Watergate crimes as ultimate consequence (White)
- **credibility-loss**: Organizational legitimacy damaged (White)
- **authority-misuse**: Power "goes to their head," gets misused (Griscom)
- **greater-good-rhetoric**: Rationalizing wrongdoing as service (White)

### 4.3 Personnel/Career
- **burnout**: Unsustainable overwork leading to exits (White, McCurry, Fetig)
- **turnover**: Staff churning through positions (Hartmann)
- **reputational-contamination**: "Being old Nixon hand was not badge of honor" (Hartmann)
- **survival-of-fittest**: Staff sorted through attrition - "side which bled most lost" (Hartmann)
- **inability-to-leave**: Staff so "intoxicated" they can't move on (Griscom)
- **physical-toll**: Lack of sleep, constant calls, long hours (Fetig)
- **leaving-for-family**: "Not lying; telling absolute truth" (Fetig)

### 4.4 Decision-Making Quality
- **delayed-recognition**: Insular staff not seeing problems until 2+ years in (Lipshutz)
- **hindsight-regret**: "Should have done something from very beginning" (Lipshutz)
- **ratings-decline-prompted-change**: Only polls forced recognition of problems (Lipshutz)
- **vulnerability-without-networks**: Lacking "wall of protection" from establishment (Lipshutz)
- **poor-pr-strategy**: Press setting narrative instead of administration (Lipshutz)
- **bush-staff-failure**: Staff not understanding how he communicated (Griscom)
- **scanner-gaffe**: Photo op failure from not complementing weaknesses (Griscom)

### 4.5 Need for Repair
- **symbolic-distancing**: Changing titles, lowering profile post-Watergate (White)
- **restoration**: Ford "helped restore respect and aura of White House" (White)
- **title-rejection**: Refusing "Director of Communications" for less controlling version (White)
- **institutional-rehabilitation**: Deliberate effort to rebuild credibility (White)
- **course-correction**: Bringing in Washington insiders late (Cutler replacing Lipshutz)
- **cutler-as-solution**: Washington insider who replaced Lipshutz (Lipshutz)
- **late-course-correction**: Multiple attempts to bring establishment figures (Lipshutz)

## 5. PROTECTIVE FACTORS (RESIST SYCOPHANCY)

### 5.1 Professional/Structural
- **peer-credibility**: Klein's journalism background giving professional respect (White)
- **professional-autonomy**: "Hired for your judgment" - authorized independently (Fetig/Clinton)
- **trust-based-system**: Professional discretion within guidelines (Fetig)
- **washington-networks-as-protection**: Insider connections providing defensive layer (Lipshutz)
- **mondale-exception**: VP as lone Washington voice with independent legitimacy (Lipshutz)
- **autonomy**: Choice to follow best practices, not mandated compliance (Lipshutz)
- **professional-self-awareness**: "We knew what we did not know" (Fetig)
- **experience-requirement**: Hiring only seasoned people (Fetig/NSC press)

### 5.2 Personal/Psychological
- **clear-principles**: Knowing one's ethical lines (White)
- **financial-independence**: Ability to leave if necessary (White, Griscom)
- **gender-marginalization**: Paradoxically, not expecting career provided protection (White)
- **moral-upbringing**: Ford's "nice boys don't lie" training (Hartmann)
- **philosophical-acceptance**: "Rolling with punches," managing uncontrollables (Lipshutz)
- **exit-readiness**: Understanding temporary nature of role (White, Griscom)
- **mission-focus**: Specific goals enabling clean exit (Griscom)
- **career-detachment**: Not expecting career as protective factor (White)

### 5.3 Leadership
- **moral-leadership**: Ford/Carter/Powell's decency setting tone (White, Mikva, Jenkins)
- **leading-by-example**: CoS and senior staff modeling ethics (White, Powell, Panetta)
- **accessible-leadership**: Ford "comfortable in his own skin" vs. needing distance (Hartmann)
- **facilitative-approach**: Enabling rather than commanding (Hartmann, White, McLarty)
- **work-life-balance**: Carter explicitly rejecting performative overwork (Lipshutz)
- **compassionate-leadership**: Powell protecting vulnerable intern (Jenkins)
- **no-personal-agenda**: Leader serving institution not self (Powell, Panetta)
- **presidential-modeling**: Carter setting reasonable hours (Lipshutz)

### 5.4 Organizational Design
- **complementary-staffing**: Hiring people unlike leader to fill gaps (Reagan-Baker per Hartmann/Griscom)
- **spokes-of-wheel**: Direct access preventing gatekeeping (Lipshutz, Hartmann)
- **no-chief-of-staff-filter**: Multiple channels to president (Lipshutz/Carter initially)
- **structural-discipline**: Panetta bringing structure without tyranny (Panetta/Clinton)
- **delegated-authority**: Baker's "no surprise rule" with accountability (Griscom)
- **adaptive-structure**: Tailoring organization to president's actual style (Griscom)
- **staff-secretary-strategic**: Paper flow control without manipulation (Panetta)

### 5.5 Decision-Making Processes
- **altruism**: "What's right for republic?" vs. "What's right for our party?" (Fetig/Clinton)
- **republic-over-party**: Prioritizing national interest over political advantage (Fetig)
- **politics-fall-away**: Seriousness when deciding for whole country (Fetig)
- **carter-self-assessment**: Reviewing own performance for "what mistakes I made" (Jenkins)
- **affirmative-action-priority**: Policy goals over personal patronage (Lipshutz)
- **principled-appointments**: Not using judgeships for political rewards (Lipshutz)
- **higher-purpose**: Serving larger cause than self or party (Fetig)

### 5.6 Relationships and Trust
- **lifelong-friendship**: Pre-political relationship enabling directness (McLarty-Clinton)
- **personal-relationship**: History creating expectation of candor (McLarty)
- **trust-foundation**: Personal relationships enabling directness (McLarty, Panetta, Lindsay)
- **trusted-professional-autonomy**: No need to check every answer with boss (Fetig)
- **do-right-thing**: Responsibility not compliance (Fetig)
- **standing-up-for-subordinate**: Protecting vulnerable staff member (Powell per Jenkins)
- **friendship-enabling-directness**: Personal relationship allowing tough feedback (McLarty)

### 5.7 Institutional Practices
- **bipartisan-cooperation**: Ford people generously helping Carter people (Lipshutz)
- **no-rigging-press-conferences**: Respecting press independence (Jenkins/Carter)
- **ethical-boundary**: Refusing to plant questions despite offers (Jenkins)
- **executive-privilege-negotiation**: Cooperation with Congress vs. confrontation (Lipshutz)
- **voluntary-deference**: Choosing to follow Ford practices based on merit (Lipshutz)
- **ford-generosity**: Outgoing administration helping successors (Lipshutz)
- **ford-carter-cooperation**: Cross-party professionalism in transition (Lipshutz)

## 8. RHETORICAL/SYMBOLIC

### 8.1 Truth and Credibility
- **stated-values**: "Truth will be hallmark" rhetoric (White/Nixon)
- **credibility-gap**: LBJ legacy creating opportunity for truth rhetoric (White)
- **truth-as-rhetoric**: Gap between stated values and actual behavior (White)
- **universal-competence**: Good decision-making same everywhere, not WH-specific (Lewis)
- **self-evident-standards**: Professional standards apply universally (Lewis)

### 8.2 Symbolic Actions
- **symbolic-language**: Title changes signaling approach shifts (White)
- **physical-barriers**: Magruder building wall to hide activities (White)

## KEY TENSIONS AND PARADOXES

### Identified Through Cross-Transition Analysis

1. **Excitement Paradox**: Can corrupt (young Nixon staff losing moral compass) OR sustain (Jenkins says never lose excitement or "don't belong there")

2. **Campaign Loyalty Paradox**: Proves commitment and tests work ethic (Lewis, Nash) OR creates entitlement and incompetence for governing (McLarty, Griscom)

3. **Friendship Paradox**: Enables directness and truth-telling (McLarty) OR creates blind spots and prevents objective assessment

4. **Spokes-of-Wheel Paradox**: Anti-hierarchical ideal preventing gatekeeping OR organizational dysfunction from lack of coordination

5. **Complementing vs. Mirroring**: Need both similarity (trust) and difference (expertise) - Reagan hiring Baker praised, but leaders naturally select similar people

6. **Service vs. Ownership**: Genuine subordination necessary OR forgetting temporary nature of role leads to entitlement (Griscom)

7. **Access Paradox**: Essential for doing job (Jenkins needing White House operator) OR intoxicating power that corrupts (Griscom on power going to heads)

8. **Trust vs. Competence**: Trust enables directness and autonomy (Lipshutz, McLarty) OR excludes necessary expertise (Lipshutz admitting mistake)

## CROSS-TRANSITION PATTERNS

### Universal Across All Transitions

1. **Campaign vs. Washington Insider Tension**: Every transition struggles with rewarding loyalists vs. bringing expertise (Ford, Carter, Reagan, Clinton)

2. **Staffing Mistakes Recognized Too Late**: Retrospective acknowledgment of errors after damage done (Hartmann, Lipshutz, Griscom, McLarty)

3. **Chief of Staff Critical for Ethical Tone**: Universally identified as key leverage point (Hartmann, White, Lewis, Griscom)

4. **Reinventing the Wheel**: Every administration's arrogance in refusing to learn from predecessors (White, Lipshutz, Griscom)

### Post-Scandal Transitions (Ford, Clinton)

- Heightened emphasis on ethics and transparency (Hartmann, White, Mikva, Cutler)
- Symbolic rejection of predecessor's worst practices (Ford/Haldeman, Clinton/polling per McCurry)
- Need to restore institutional credibility (White, Cutler)

### Outsider Transitions (Carter, Clinton)

- Lacking Washington networks makes vulnerable to establishment (Lipshutz, Lewis)
- "Mafia" phenomenon (Georgia, Arkansas) of insular trust-based staffing (Lipshutz, Mikva)
- Recognition of mistake only after damage done (Lipshutz, McLarty)

### Mid-Term Transitions (Ford succession, Reagan-Baker)

- Job insecurity driving sycophantic behavior as staff compete for new leader's favor (Hartmann, Griscom)
- Tension between continuity and change (Hartmann, Griscom)
- Opportunity to correct earlier mistakes (Griscom)
